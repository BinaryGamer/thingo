Stephen Hawking (1942 - 2018)

Stephen Hawking was an English theoretical physicist and cosmologist. He is best known for his study of black holes and the discovery that they can emit radiation particles, often called Hawking radiation, even though their immense gravitational attraction means that no matter or energy can escape from inside them.

Life

Stephen Hawking was born on 8 January 1942 in Oxford, England. He studied science at Oxford University, and then at Cambridge.

While Hawking was studying he was diagnosed with ALS, and told that he had just two years to live. However, the disease progressed more slowly than the doctors predicted. Hawking went on to have a successful career in science, making major contributions to our understanding of the origins of the universe and the nature of black holes.

Hawking also got married and had three children. He became a famous figure, published a bestselling book called A Brief History of Time, and even appeared on The Simpsons and Star Trek. What is ALS?

ALS stands for amyotrophic lateral sclerosis. It is also known as motor neurone disease, or MND. ALS is a degenerative disease that paralyses the muscles over time.

As his disease progressed, Hawking lost the ability to walk and talk. He started using a wheelchair to move and a computer to communicate by moving muscles in his cheek.

However, even though ALS paralysed Hawking's body, his mind remained sharp.
